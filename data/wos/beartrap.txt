The Bear Trap in Whiteout Survival is the focus of the Bear Hunt, an alliance event that offers substantial rewards based on the total damage dealt to a Raging Bear. Maximizing damage requires strategic coordination, hero selection, and troop composition. 
Event Mechanics and Requirements
Availability: The Bear Hunt is an alliance event that R4/R5 members can activate approximately every two days and lasts for 30 minutes.
Building: The Hunting Trap must be built within Alliance Territory, which requires the alliance to have an HQ (minimum of 20 members). There are specific material costs for building the trap at Alliance Level 5 and 6.
Cooldown: Each player has a personal cooldown of two "natural days" (game day resets at 00:00 UTC) after participating in a bear hunt rally, separate from the trap's cooldown.
Enhancement: Before starting the hunt, alliance members can donate Explosive Arrowheads (obtained from hunting beasts) to enhance the trap up to level 5, providing a 25% attack boost for all participants. This enhancement resets after the event.
Rewards: Rewards are given based on a player's personal damage contribution and include resources (Meat, Wood, Coal), Alliance Tokens, Essence Stones, and Gear XP. 
Maximizing Damage (Strategy)
The goal is to deal maximum damage within the 30-minute time limit by launching and joining as many rallies as possible. 
Rally Leaders' Positioning: Rally leaders should teleport their cities as close as possible to the Bear Trap to minimize march time and maximize the number of rallies they can initiate within the time limit.
Rally Leader Heroes: Leaders should use their three strongest heroes, as all three of their Expedition skills (damage buffs) are active for the entire rally. Jeronimo, Bahiti, Jessie, and Natalia are top choices.
Rally Joiner Heroes: Joiners should place a specific hero in the first (leftmost) position of their march. The best heroes for this role are Jessie, Jasser, Seo-yoon, or Jeronimo, as their first Expedition skill provides a rally damage buff to the entire rally. If you run out of suitable heroes, send no hero at all, only troops, to avoid potentially using a hero with a low-level or incorrect skill that could overwrite a better buff.
Troop Composition: Marksmen (Ranged troops) deal the most damage to the bear. A recommended ratio is around 10% Infantry, 30% Lancers, and 60% Marksmen (or even higher Marksmen percentages like 5/5/90 if possible). The bear does not fight back, so defense stats are not a concern.
Buffs: Utilize Settlement buffs (Troop Attack, March Capacity), complete Weapons Analysis Center research, and use any available march speed boosts (e.g., Expedition Facility, Snow Leopard pet skill) to optimize performance.
Coordination: Join rallies led by the strongest players in your alliance. Pre-save your ideal troop formations to quickly join rallies during the event. 
Important Tips
Do not claim the rewards mail immediately if there is a war going on in your state; the resources are safe in the mail for a few days, but vulnerable to looting once claimed into your city.
Each bear trap has separate enhancement progress, so you must donate arrowheads to both if your alliance uses two traps.
You can still join up to four rallies even if you can only send one personal march, by managing your rally call and joins effectively

1.What is the single most important troop type for maximizing damage against the Raging Bear?
The single most important troop type for maximizing damage against the Raging Bear is the Marksman (also known as Ranged troops or Archers).
The strategy is centered on this troop type for a very specific reason: the Raging Bear in the Bear Hunt event is a unique boss that does not fight back or cause troop injuries.
Therefore, defensive stats like Troop Defense and Troop Health are irrelevant. Players can completely focus on optimizing for raw damage output, and Marksmen consistently provide the highest damage per troop against this particular target compared to Infantry or Lancers. By heavily favoring Marksmen in the troop composition (up to 80-90% of the march), players maximize the total damage dealt within the 30-minute time limit.


2.What are the optimal troop ratios (Infantry/Lancers/Marksmen) recommended for bear trap rallies, and why are these specific ratios used?

The optimal troop ratios for the Whiteout Survival Bear Trap heavily prioritize Marksmen, with common recommendations being 10% Infantry, 10% Lancers, and 80% Marksmen or even higher Marksmen percentages like 5% Infantry, 5% Lancers, and 90% Marksmen. 
Why These Ratios Are Used
Marksmen for Maximum Damage: The primary reason is that Marksmen have the highest base Attack and Lethality stats among all troop types, and in the Bear Hunt event, the bear does not attack back. This means defensive stats (Health and Defense) are irrelevant, and the focus is purely on raw damage output.
Small Numbers of Infantry and Lancers: Infantry and Lancers are included in small numbers (around 10% each) for two main reasons:
Hero Skill Activation: A small number of these troops are necessary to ensure that all of the rally leader's three hero skills are fully activated and provide their powerful buffs. Some alliance tests suggest that using at least 10,000 of each troop type can prevent a damage reduction bug, although this is debated.
Stat Optimization: While Marksmen deal the most damage, sending a few Infantry and Lancers in a multi-march strategy (using all available troops across several rallies) is more effective than leaving these troops idle in your city, as some damage is better than none.
Avoid Equal Distribution: Using an equal distribution (e.g., 33/33/33) is not recommended because Infantry are the least effective damage dealers against the bear. 
The specific ratio a player uses may vary slightly based on their individual troop counts, hero levels, and available Chief Gear, but the core principle remains: maximize the number of Marksmen while ensuring all hero skills are active.

6.How do you know which four joiners' skills are active in a rally, and what happens if a player joins with an unoptimized hero?

In a rally, only up to four joining players' first Expedition skills can provide a bonus to the entire group. The game selects these four buffs based on a priority system. 
How the Joiner Skills Are Prioritized
The game's selection process for which four skills are active follows these rules:
Highest Skill Level First: The game prioritizes the first Expedition skills with the highest level (e.g., Level 5 is chosen over Level 4).
Rarity Tie-breaker: If multiple players have skills of the same level, heroes of higher rarity (Mythic > Epic > Elite) are prioritized.
First to Join Tie-breaker: If skill levels and rarity are still equal, the player who joined the rally earlier is prioritized. 
What Happens If a Player Joins with an Unoptimized Hero
Joining a rally with an unoptimized hero (e.g., a low-level skill, a defensive hero, or a hero with a chance-based skill) can negatively impact the entire rally in the following ways:
Wasting a Buff Slot: If a player joins early with a Level 5 skill that is either irrelevant to damage (like a defensive skill) or not ideal for the bear hunt (like a stun chance), they will take up one of the four valuable buff slots. This prevents a more optimal damage-boosting skill from a later joiner from being used.
Lowering Overall Damage: Using an ineffective hero means the rally is missing out on a potentially large damage increase (e.g., 25% damage boost from a Jessie). This results in lower total damage for the alliance and lower personal damage scores for everyone in that specific rally.
The "No Hero" Option: If a player runs out of appropriate damage-boosting heroes (Jessie, Jasser, etc.), it is often recommended to send their troops with no hero at all as a last resort. This prevents them from occupying one of the four precious buff slots with a useless skill, allowing a later joiner with an optimal hero to have their skill included instead. The only downside is a potential reduction in march capacity for that player's specific march. 


7.What is the purpose of donating "Explosive Arrowheads" before the hunt begins, and what is the maximum attack bonus achievable?

The purpose of donating Explosive Arrowheads is to enhance the Bear Trap and provide a significant, alliance-wide damage bonus against the Raging Bear.
Purpose of Donating Explosive Arrowheads
Damage Boost: Donating Arrowheads increases the "Enhancement Level" of the Hunting Trap. Each level provides a cumulative attack bonus for all alliance members who participate in rallies against that specific bear trap.
Source: Explosive Arrowheads are primarily earned by completing Lighthouse Intel Missions.
Alliance Tokens: Donating Arrowheads also rewards the individual player with Alliance Tokens (200 tokens per arrowhead donated), which can be used to purchase items from the Alliance Shop. 
Maximum Attack Bonus Achievable
The bear trap can be enhanced up to Level 5. At this maximum level, it provides a total bonus of 25% increased Troop Attack for all participants in the bear hunt:

8.Why should rally leaders and joiners teleport their cities close to the Bear Trap location during the event?
Both rally leaders and joiners should teleport their cities as close to the Bear Trap as possible during the event for one critical reason: time optimization. 
The Importance of Speed
The entire Bear Hunt event has a strict time limit of just 30 minutes. The goal for both the alliance and individual players is to launch and participate in as many rallies as possible within this short window to maximize total damage and rewards.
Minimizing March Time: By reducing the physical distance troops need to travel, march time is significantly shortened. Shorter march times mean troops spend less time walking across the map and more time attacking the bear.
Maximizing Rally Frequency:
For Joiners: Faster return times mean players can join subsequent rallies sooner, increasing their total number of attacks.
For Leaders: Shorter distances enable leaders to launch the maximum number of 5-minute rallies within the event's timeframe (up to five optimally timed rallies are possible with very short march times).
Efficiency: The total damage output is a function of both the strength of each march and the number of marches launched. Minimizing travel time is a key factor in boosting the frequency of attacks and overall efficiency. 
Alliance R4 and R5 members often coordinate a "hive" formation where everyone is tightly packed around the trap to ensure all members can move quickly and efficiently.

10.How does the "personal cooldown" work for participating in the Bear Hunt event after joining a rally?
The personal cooldown mechanism is a critical feature designed to prevent individual players from participating in multiple Whiteout Survival Bear Hunt events within a short timeframe, thereby ensuring fairness across alliances.
How the Personal Cooldown Works
Activation: The cooldown is triggered immediately after a player participates in any bear trap rally (either by leading or joining).
Duration: The cooldown lasts for approximately 48 hours.
Reset Mechanism: The cooldown does not count down in real-time minutes or hours; it is based on the "natural game day" resets.
The in-game day typically resets at 00:00 UTC (Universal Coordinated Time).
If you participate in a bear hunt on Day 1, your cooldown will last through the end of Day 2. You will be clear to participate in the next event starting on Day 3 after the 00:00 UTC reset.
Key Implications
One Event Per Player (within the window): A player can only participate in one Bear Hunt event initiated by any alliance (their own or another's) within that specific two-day window.
Alliance Planning: Alliances typically activate a second bear trap the day after the first one to maximize overall alliance points for the week, but they must use different sets of players for each event due to this cooldown.
Avoiding "Cooldown Traps": Players must coordinate with their alliance leaders to ensure they only join their assigned bear hunt, as accidentally joining a rogue or alternative alliance's trap will trigger the cooldown and prevent them from participating in their main alliance's scheduled event.
